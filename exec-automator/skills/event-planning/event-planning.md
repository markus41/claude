# Event Planning & Management

Expert knowledge in association event management, conference planning, and educational programming. This skill provides comprehensive guidance on planning, executing, and evaluating successful association events from small webinars to large-scale annual conferences.

## Core Competencies

### Event Types
- Annual conferences and conventions
- Regional meetings and chapter events
- Educational webinars and virtual sessions
- Hybrid events (in-person + virtual)
- Board retreats and leadership summits
- Certification ceremonies and awards galas
- Trade shows and exhibitions
- Continuing education workshops
- Member networking events
- Advocacy days and hill visits

### Planning Timeline Framework

#### 12-18 Months Pre-Event
- Define event goals and objectives
- Establish budget parameters
- Select event dates (check for conflicts)
- Issue RFP for venues (for major conferences)
- Form planning committee
- Identify potential keynote speakers
- Determine educational tracks/themes
- Create high-level marketing timeline

#### 9-12 Months Pre-Event
- Finalize venue contract
- Negotiate hotel room blocks
- Develop preliminary program outline
- Issue speaker/presentation call for proposals
- Secure keynote speakers
- Launch event website/landing page
- Begin sponsor prospecting
- Identify AV and production needs
- Plan virtual platform requirements (hybrid events)

#### 6-9 Months Pre-Event
- Review and select breakout sessions
- Finalize educational program schedule
- Launch early bird registration
- Begin marketing campaign (email, social, ads)
- Open exhibitor/sponsor sales
- Confirm CE/CEU accreditation requirements
- Develop mobile app specifications
- Create promotional materials
- Book entertainment/special events
- Arrange catering and special dietary needs

#### 3-6 Months Pre-Event
- Send speaker confirmation and guidelines
- Ramp up marketing efforts
- Monitor registration pace vs. projections
- Finalize exhibitor floor plan
- Coordinate with speakers on AV needs
- Create attendee engagement strategy
- Plan networking activities
- Develop on-site signage and materials
- Confirm all vendor contracts
- Create event timeline/run of show

#### 1-3 Months Pre-Event
- Send regular updates to registrants
- Conduct speaker preparation sessions
- Finalize all logistics (transportation, meals, etc.)
- Create name badges and attendee materials
- Prepare volunteer/staff schedules
- Test virtual platform thoroughly
- Submit CE/CEU documentation
- Create post-event survey
- Develop social media content calendar
- Confirm final attendance numbers with venue

#### 2-4 Weeks Pre-Event
- Send pre-event communications to attendees
- Provide final details to speakers
- Conduct venue walkthrough
- Test all AV equipment
- Prepare registration desk procedures
- Create staff communication plan
- Finalize mobile app content
- Send virtual attendee access instructions
- Prepare contingency plans
- Review emergency procedures

#### Event Week
- Set up registration area
- Conduct tech rehearsals
- Brief staff and volunteers
- Monitor virtual platform performance
- Manage day-of logistics
- Facilitate networking opportunities
- Capture content (photos, videos, quotes)
- Monitor social media engagement
- Handle real-time issues
- Collect feedback continuously

#### Post-Event (1-4 Weeks)
- Send thank you messages
- Distribute post-event survey
- Provide CE/CEU certificates
- Share session recordings/materials
- Compile attendance and engagement metrics
- Conduct debrief with planning team
- Process final vendor invoices
- Archive event materials
- Begin planning for next year
- Report results to board/leadership

## Venue Selection & Management

### Key Venue Criteria
- Location accessibility (airports, transportation)
- Meeting space capacity and configuration
- Technology infrastructure (Wi-Fi, AV)
- Hotel room block availability
- Food and beverage options
- Accessibility compliance (ADA)
- Cost competitiveness
- Previous client references
- Climate and seasonal considerations
- Local attractions and networking venues

### Contract Negotiation Points
- Room rental fees and concessions
- Attrition clauses (room blocks and F&B)
- Cancellation policies and force majeure
- Audio-visual equipment included
- Internet access (bandwidth and cost)
- Setup and teardown times
- Security and insurance requirements
- Parking arrangements
- Shipping and storage procedures
- Payment terms and schedules

### Site Visit Checklist
- Inspect all meeting rooms and layouts
- Test Wi-Fi and connectivity
- Review accessibility features
- Evaluate registration space
- Check exhibit hall setup options
- Assess food service areas
- Examine breakout room locations
- Review loading dock access
- Verify signage opportunities
- Tour hotel rooms and amenities

## Speaker & Content Management

### Speaker Recruitment Strategy
- Define speaker qualifications and expertise
- Create compelling call for proposals (CFP)
- Establish review criteria and rubric
- Form diverse selection committee
- Provide clear submission guidelines
- Set realistic submission deadlines
- Offer speaker incentives (comp registration, travel)
- Communicate selection timeline
- Provide feedback to declined proposals

### Speaker Agreement Essentials
- Presentation title and description
- Session date, time, and duration
- AV and technical requirements
- Content ownership and recording rights
- Slide template and branding guidelines
- Submission deadlines for materials
- Continuing education disclosures
- Cancellation policies
- Compensation or reimbursement terms
- Code of conduct acknowledgment

### Content Development Support
- Provide presentation templates
- Share learning objectives framework
- Offer design resources
- Conduct speaker orientation sessions
- Review slides for quality and compliance
- Ensure accessibility standards (alt text, captions)
- Test interactive elements
- Prepare backup copies of presentations
- Create speaker resource kit
- Assign speaker liaisons for day-of support

### Session Format Options
- Keynote presentations (60-90 min)
- Educational sessions (45-60 min)
- Panel discussions (60-75 min)
- Interactive workshops (90-120 min)
- Lightning talks (15-20 min)
- Poster sessions (self-guided)
- Roundtable discussions (30-45 min)
- Fireside chats (45-60 min)
- Case study presentations (30-45 min)
- Live demonstrations (30-60 min)

## Continuing Education (CE/CEU) Administration

### Accreditation Process
- Identify relevant accrediting bodies
- Submit applications 60-90 days in advance
- Provide required documentation
  - Learning objectives
  - Instructor qualifications
  - Content outlines
  - Assessment methods
- Pay accreditation fees
- Display accreditation statements
- Track application status
- Maintain accreditation records

### Common Accrediting Bodies
- NASBA (CPE for CPAs)
- AMA PRA (Physicians)
- ANCC (Nurses)
- ASHA (Speech-Language Pathologists)
- HRCI and SHRM (HR Professionals)
- State bar associations (Attorneys)
- AIA (Architects)
- IACET (CEUs)
- PMI (Project Management)
- Industry-specific boards

### Compliance Requirements
- Sign-in/sign-out tracking
- Minimum attendance thresholds (typically 80%)
- Learning assessments or evaluations
- Conflict of interest disclosures
- Commercial support disclosure
- Certificate of completion issuance
- Record retention (typically 4-7 years)
- Participant verification procedures
- Attendance monitoring technology
- Make-up work policies (if applicable)

### Certificate Management
- Automated certificate generation
- Personalized with attendee name
- Include credit hours/units
- Display accreditation logos
- Provide digital and printable formats
- Email delivery within specified timeframe
- Duplicate certificate requests process
- Secure storage and retrieval
- Tracking and reporting tools
- Integration with learning management systems

## Virtual & Hybrid Event Technology

### Platform Selection Criteria
- Attendee capacity and scalability
- Interactive features (polls, Q&A, chat)
- Breakout room capabilities
- Networking tools (1:1 meetings, lounges)
- Mobile app availability
- Integration with registration system
- Analytics and reporting
- Recording and on-demand access
- Accessibility features (captions, translations)
- Technical support availability
- Security and privacy features
- Cost structure (per attendee, flat fee, etc.)

### Popular Virtual Event Platforms
- Zoom Events/Webinar
- Hopin
- Cvent Virtual
- vFairs
- Whova
- EventMobi
- ON24
- RingCentral Events
- Microsoft Teams Live Events
- Webex Events

### Virtual Event Best Practices
- Conduct platform training for speakers and staff
- Create clear attendee instructions
- Test all technology in advance
- Provide technical support during event
- Engage attendees with interactive elements
- Monitor chat and Q&A actively
- Record sessions for on-demand viewing
- Offer virtual networking opportunities
- Create branded virtual environment
- Provide presentation templates optimized for screen
- Schedule breaks to avoid screen fatigue
- Time zone considerations for global audience

### Hybrid Event Considerations
- Parity of experience (in-person vs. virtual)
- Dedicated production team for virtual component
- Camera placement and audio quality
- Slide sharing and presentation sync
- Interactive elements for both audiences
- Networking opportunities for virtual attendees
- Speaker preparation for dual audience
- Registration management for both formats
- Pricing strategy (differential or equal)
- Technology redundancy and backup plans

## Budget Management

### Revenue Sources
- Registration fees
- Sponsorship and exhibitor sales
- Grants or underwriting
- Merchandise sales
- Post-event content sales
- Advertising in program or app
- Hotel commissions (if applicable)
- Job board or career fair fees

### Major Expense Categories
- Venue rental and catering (typically 40-50%)
- Audio-visual and production (10-15%)
- Marketing and promotion (5-10%)
- Speaker expenses (travel, honorariums) (5-10%)
- Registration platform and technology (5-8%)
- Printing and materials (3-5%)
- Staff and temporary labor (5-10%)
- Insurance and security (2-3%)
- Virtual platform (for hybrid events) (5-10%)
- Miscellaneous and contingency (5-10%)

### Budget Tracking Best Practices
- Use detailed line-item budget template
- Track estimated vs. actual costs
- Update budget regularly (weekly during event prep)
- Monitor registration revenue vs. projections
- Establish approval thresholds for expenses
- Maintain vendor contract repository
- Track payment schedules
- Reconcile invoices promptly post-event
- Calculate ROI and cost-per-attendee metrics
- Create budget summary for leadership

### Cost-Saving Strategies
- Negotiate venue concessions
- Partner with local CVB for marketing support
- Leverage member volunteers
- Use digital program vs. print
- Bundle AV needs for discounts
- Early booking discounts
- Year-over-year venue commitments
- Sponsorship for specific line items
- Virtual options to reduce space needs
- Compare vendor quotes competitively
