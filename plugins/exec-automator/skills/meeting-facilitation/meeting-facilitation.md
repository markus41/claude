# Meeting Facilitation & Governance Expertise

This skill provides comprehensive knowledge of meeting management, Robert's Rules of Order, board governance protocols, and professional facilitation techniques for nonprofit and corporate boards.

## Core Competencies

### 1. Robert's Rules of Order Fundamentals

**Basic Principles:**
- One subject at a time
- Full and free discussion of every motion
- All members have equal rights and responsibilities
- Majority rules, minority protected
- Proper procedures ensure fair decision-making

**Order of Precedence for Motions:**

1. **Privileged Motions** (highest precedence)
   - Fix the time to which to adjourn
   - Adjourn
   - Recess
   - Question of privilege
   - Call for orders of the day

2. **Subsidiary Motions**
   - Lay on the table
   - Previous question (close debate)
   - Limit or extend debate
   - Postpone to a certain time
   - Commit or refer
   - Amend
   - Postpone indefinitely

3. **Main Motions** (lowest precedence)
   - General main motions
   - Specific main motions

**Incidental Motions:**
- Point of order
- Appeal
- Suspend the rules
- Object to consideration
- Division of a question
- Division of the assembly
- Parliamentary inquiry
- Request for information

**Motions That Bring a Question Again Before the Assembly:**
- Take from the table
- Rescind
- Amend something previously adopted
- Reconsider

### 2. Board Meeting Protocols

**Pre-Meeting Requirements:**

**Notice Requirements:**
- Regular meetings: As specified in bylaws (typically 7-14 days)
- Special meetings: Minimum notice as required by bylaws
- Emergency meetings: Reasonable notice under circumstances
- Annual meetings: 30-60 days advance notice recommended

**Quorum Determination:**
- Definition: Minimum number of members required for valid business
- Standard quorum: Majority of board members unless bylaws specify otherwise
- Quorum must be present when vote is taken
- Loss of quorum requires adjournment or recess
- Virtual attendance counts if permitted by bylaws

**Meeting Opening Sequence:**
1. Call to order by presiding officer
2. Establish quorum (Secretary reports)
3. Approve agenda (as needed)
4. Approve previous meeting minutes
5. Officers' reports
6. Committee reports
7. Old/unfinished business
8. New business
9. Announcements
10. Adjournment

### 3. Agenda Development

**Standard Board Meeting Agenda Template:**

```
BOARD OF DIRECTORS MEETING AGENDA
[Organization Name]
[Date, Time, Location]

I. CALL TO ORDER
   - Roll call and quorum verification

II. APPROVAL OF AGENDA
   - Motion to approve/amend agenda

III. CONSENT AGENDA
   - Previous meeting minutes
   - Financial reports
   - Routine approvals
   - Correspondence
   Motion to approve consent agenda as presented

IV. REPORTS
   A. Executive Director's Report
   B. Financial Report (Treasurer)
   C. Committee Reports
      1. Finance Committee
      2. Development Committee
      3. Program Committee
      4. Governance Committee
   D. Special Reports

V. OLD BUSINESS
   A. [Item from previous meeting]
   B. [Follow-up items]

VI. NEW BUSINESS
   A. [Strategic items requiring discussion]
   B. [Policy considerations]
   C. [Major decisions]

VII. EXECUTIVE SESSION (if needed)
   - Personnel matters
   - Legal issues
   - Real estate negotiations

VIII. ANNOUNCEMENTS
   - Next meeting date
   - Upcoming events
   - Committee meetings

IX. ADJOURNMENT
   - Motion to adjourn

Time allocated: [X] minutes per section
```

**Consent Agenda Best Practices:**
- Group routine, non-controversial items
- Distribute materials in advance
- Allow any member to pull items for discussion
- Single motion approves all items
- Saves time for strategic discussion

**Timed Agenda Format:**
```
3:00 PM - Call to Order
3:05 PM - Consent Agenda Approval
3:10 PM - Executive Director Report (15 min)
3:25 PM - Financial Report (10 min)
3:35 PM - Committee Reports (20 min)
3:55 PM - Strategic Discussion: [Topic] (30 min)
4:25 PM - New Business (20 min)
4:45 PM - Announcements & Adjournment
```

**Minutes Writing Standards:**
- Record what was done, not what was said
- Capture all motions verbatim with maker and seconder
- Record vote results (unanimous, majority, specific count)
- Summarize discussion points without attributing statements
- Note time of executive session entry/exit
- Include all required procedural actions
- Avoid editorial comments or interpretation
- Use past tense and third person
- Proofread before distribution
- Archive approved minutes permanently

### 5. Motion Processing Procedures

**Standard Motion Format:**
"I move that [specific action to be taken]."

**Motion Handling Steps:**

1. **Member makes motion**
   - Chair: "It has been moved by [Name] that [restate motion]."
   - Confirm motion is understood and properly stated

2. **Second required**
   - Chair: "Is there a second?"
   - Another member: "I second the motion."
   - Chair: "It has been moved and seconded that [restate motion]."
   - If no second: "The motion fails for lack of a second."

3. **Discussion/Debate**
   - Chair opens floor for discussion
   - Maker of motion speaks first
   - Alternate between pro and con speakers
   - Chair facilitates balanced discussion
   - Members address remarks to chair, not each other
   - Stay on topic of pending motion

4. **Amendments (if offered)**
   - "I move to amend the motion by [addition/deletion/substitution]."
   - Amendment requires second
   - Discuss and vote on amendment first
   - Return to main motion (as amended or original)

5. **Close Debate**
   - Chair: "Are you ready for the question?"
   - Or member moves previous question (requires 2/3 vote)
   - Restate the motion clearly

6. **Vote**
   - Chair: "All those in favor, say 'aye.'"
   - "All those opposed, say 'no.'"
   - "The ayes/noes have it, and the motion is adopted/defeated."

7. **Announcement of Result**
   - State whether motion passed or failed
   - Indicate what action will be taken
   - Record in minutes

**Vote Requirements by Motion Type:**

| Motion Type | Vote Required | Debatable | Amendable |
|------------|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| Main motion | Majority | Yes | Yes |
| Amend | Majority | Yes | Yes |
| Commit/Refer | Majority | Yes | Yes |
| Postpone to certain time | Majority | Yes | Yes |
| Previous question | 2/3 | No | No |
| Limit debate | 2/3 | No | Yes |
| Suspend rules | 2/3 | No | No |
| Adjourn | Majority | No | No |
| Recess | Majority | No | Yes |

### 6. Voting Procedures

**Voting Methods:**

1. **Voice Vote (Viva Voce)**
   - Standard method for most motions
   - "All in favor say 'aye'; opposed say 'no'"
   - Chair judges volume
   - Any member can request division if unclear

2. **Show of Hands/Rising Vote**
   - Used when voice vote is unclear
   - Provides visual count
   - Secretary counts and records

3. **Roll Call Vote**
   - Each member's vote recorded individually
   - Used for important or controversial matters
   - Creates permanent record
   - Alphabetical order typically

4. **Ballot Vote**
   - Secret written vote
   - Used for elections or sensitive matters
   - Tellers count and report
   - Protects voter privacy

5. **Unanimous Consent**
   - Chair: "Is there any objection to [action]?"
   - If no objection, action is approved
   - One objection requires formal vote
   - Efficient for routine matters

**Vote Counting Rules:**
- **Majority Vote:** More than half of votes cast (not members present)
- **2/3 Vote:** At least two-thirds of votes cast
- **Abstentions:** Do not count as votes cast
- **Tie Vote:** Motion fails (except chair can break tie if permitted)

**Conflict of Interest Protocol:**
1. Member discloses potential conflict
2. Board discusses whether conflict exists
3. Conflicted member leaves room
4. Board discusses and votes
5. Conflicted member returns
6. Decision announced

### 7. Executive Session Protocols

**Valid Reasons for Executive Session:**
- Personnel matters (hiring, evaluation, discipline)
- Legal matters (litigation, attorney advice)
- Real estate negotiations
- Security matters
- Donor privacy issues
- Contract negotiations

**Executive Session Procedures:**

1. **Entry into Executive Session**
   - Requires motion stating specific purpose
   - Cannot state "executive session" without reason
   - Motion must pass by majority
   - Non-board members excused unless invited
   - Record time of entry in minutes

2. **During Executive Session**
   - Discussion limited to stated purpose
   - No minutes taken (some exceptions by state law)
   - Maintain strict confidentiality
   - No decisions unless absolutely necessary
   - Keep discussion focused

3. **Return to Open Session**
   - Motion to return to open session
   - Record time of return
   - Any votes taken must be ratified in open session
   - No disclosure of confidential discussion

4. **Follow-up Documentation**
   - Minutes note: "Board entered executive session at [time] to discuss [general category]. Returned to open session at [time]. No action taken."
   - Maintain separate confidential notes if required by state law
   - Document any votes in open session minutes

### 8. Action Item Tracking

**Action Item Documentation Format:**

```
ACTION ITEMS - [Meeting Date]

Item #1: [Brief Description]
Responsible: [Name]
Deadline: [Date]
Status: Not Started / In Progress / Completed
Notes: [Additional context]

Item #2: [Brief Description]
Responsible: [Name]
Deadline: [Date]
Status: In Progress
Notes: Awaiting [dependency]
```

**Action Item Management Best Practices:**
- Assign specific individuals (not committees)
- Set clear deadlines
- Include in next meeting agenda
- Send reminders before deadline
- Report status at each meeting
- Close completed items formally

### 9. Special Meeting Scenarios

**Emergency Meetings:**
- Minimum notice as reasonable under circumstances
- Limited to emergency business only
- Document urgency in minutes
- Follow up with regular meeting

**Virtual Meetings:**
- Ensure bylaws permit virtual participation
- Test technology before meeting
- Confirm all can hear and be heard
- Use roll call votes for clarity
- Record meeting if permitted
- Provide alternative participation methods

**Committee Meetings:**
- Less formal than full board
- Still require notice and minutes
- Report to full board
- No authority to bind organization without board approval
- Follow same basic parliamentary procedures

**Annual Meetings:**
- Fulfill state law requirements
- Elect officers/directors
- Present annual report
- Review year's activities
- Set strategic direction
- Higher formality level

### 10. Facilitation Techniques

**Effective Chair Practices:**

1. **Pre-Meeting Preparation**
   - Review all materials thoroughly
   - Consult with Executive Director
   - Anticipate controversial items
   - Prepare time allocations
   - Identify speakers/presenters

2. **During Meeting**
   - Start and end on time
   - Keep discussion focused
   - Ensure all voices heard
   - Manage dominating personalities
   - Restate motions clearly
   - Summarize decisions
   - Maintain neutrality

3. **Challenging Situations**
   - **Sidebar Conversations:** "Let's have one conversation."
   - **Off-Topic Discussion:** "That's important, but let's table for later."
   - **Dominating Member:** "Thank you. Let's hear from others."
   - **Unclear Motion:** "Let me restate to ensure clarity."
   - **Heated Debate:** "Let's take a brief recess."

**Discussion Management:**
- Use "parking lot" for off-topic items
- Employ round-robin for balanced input
- Set time limits on discussion
- Summarize before voting
- Acknowledge emotions while maintaining order

**Decision-Making Frameworks:**

1. **Consensus Building**
   - Seek areas of agreement
   - Address concerns systematically
   - Test for consensus before formal vote
   - "Can everyone live with this?"

2. **Straw Polls**
   - Informal sense of group
   - Not binding
   - Helps gauge support
   - Identifies issues to address

3. **Parking Lot Technique**
   - Capture important but off-agenda items
   - Promise future consideration
   - Maintain focus on current business
   - Review at meeting end

## Key Principles Summary

1. **Fairness:** All members have equal voice and vote
2. **Efficiency:** Accomplish business expeditiously
3. **Clarity:** Ensure everyone understands proceedings
4. **Documentation:** Create permanent, accurate record
5. **Respect:** Maintain civility and decorum
6. **Transparency:** Conduct business openly (except executive session)
7. **Accountability:** Track decisions and actions
8. **Compliance:** Follow bylaws, laws, and best practices

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

- Making decisions without proper motion/vote
- Failing to establish quorum
- Allowing discussion before motion is seconded
- Voting on unclear motions
- Inadequate notice for meetings
- Poor minute-keeping (too detailed or too sparse)
- Misuse of executive session
- Ignoring conflicts of interest
- Allowing procedural shortcuts that undermine governance
- Failing to follow up on action items
