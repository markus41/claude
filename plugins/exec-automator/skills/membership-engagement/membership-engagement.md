### 6. Lapsed Member Win-Back

#### Lapsed Member Segmentation

**Recently Lapsed (0-6 Months):**
- High win-back probability (35-45%)
- Strategy: Remind of value, offer discount
- Tactic: "We miss you" campaign with 25% discount

**Medium Lapsed (6-12 Months):**
- Medium win-back probability (15-25%)
- Strategy: Highlight new benefits since they left
- Tactic: Showcase member success stories, offer trial period

- Low win-back probability (5-10%)

### 8. Member Satisfaction Measurement

#### Survey Strategies

**Annual Comprehensive Survey (20-30 questions):**
- Overall satisfaction (NPS)
- Benefit utilization and value
- Event quality ratings
- Communication effectiveness
- Likelihood to renew
- Demographic and business data
- Open-ended improvement suggestions

**Quarterly Pulse Surveys (3-5 questions):**
- What benefit did you use this quarter?
- How satisfied were you with [Recent Event]?
- On a scale of 1-10, how likely are you to renew?
- What's one thing we could improve?

**Post-Event Surveys (5-7 questions):**
- Event rating (1-5 stars)
- Content quality
- Networking opportunities
- Logistics (venue, timing, food)
- Speaker effectiveness
- Likelihood to attend future events
- Testimonial request

#### Net Promoter Score (NPS)

**Question:**
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [Organization] membership to a colleague?"

**Scoring:**
- 9-10: Promoters (loyal enthusiasts)
- 7-8: Passives (satisfied but unenthusiastic)
- 0-6: Detractors (unhappy, at-risk)

**NPS Calculation:**
NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors

**Industry Benchmarks:**
- Excellent: 50+
- Good: 30-50
- Average: 10-30
- Needs Improvement: <10

**Follow-Up Actions:**
- Promoters: Request testimonial, ask for referrals
- Passives: Identify what would make them promoters
- Detractors: Personal outreach, service recovery
